\section{Analytical Tasks}

\textbf{Analytical Tasks.}
Based on the analytical needs identified in Tree Evolution analysis, we derive the following tasks:

\begin{itemize}
	\item \textbf{T1: Compare adjacent trees and identify structural changes.}
	Analysts need to examine how one tree transforms into the next, including inserted, deleted, relabeled, or reorganized structures, while preserving topology awareness.
	
	\item \textbf{T2: Interpret critical differences in structural context.}
	Analysts need to focus on important local changes, while retaining the surrounding hierarchical context necessary to understand their meanings and impacts.
	
	\item \textbf{T3: Summarize long evolution sequences and locate key transition steps.}
	Analysts need to obtain an overview of long tree evolution processes, identify salient intervals and major transition points, and trace how local changes accumulate into global evolution patterns.
\end{itemize}
